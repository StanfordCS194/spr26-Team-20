PRINTIMATE — PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: May 14, 2026
Effective date: May 14, 2026


1. WHO WE ARE

Printimate is a mobile and web app developed by the Printimate team, a student
project from Stanford University's CS 194W course. In this policy, "we," "us,"
and "our" refer to the Printimate team. "You" refers to anyone who uses the
Printimate app or service.

If you have questions about this policy or want to exercise any of the rights
described below, you can reach us at:

    printimately@gmail.com


2. SCOPE

This policy describes the personal data we collect when you use the Printimate
mobile app (Android and iOS), the Printimate web app, and the Printimate
backend service (collectively, "the Service"). It applies to all users of the
Service worldwide.

The Service is not directed to children under 13 years of age. We do not
knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe
a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact
us at printimately@gmail.com and we will delete it.


3. WHAT THE SERVICE DOES

Printimate lets you send short messages — text, photos, or hand-drawn
sketches — that print on a paired physical thermal printer. To make this
work, the Service needs to know who you are, who you're allowed to send
messages to, and which printer your messages should print on.


4. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

4.1 Information you give us directly

  - Account information: when you sign in, we receive your email address,
    display name, and (if available) profile photo URL from your chosen
    sign-in provider (Google, Apple, or email/password).

  - Message content: the text, photos, and drawings you send through the
    app. Photos may come from your camera or your device's photo library
    depending on which option you choose at the moment of sending.

  - Printer pairing information: the unique identifier of any printer you
    pair with your account, and your role with respect to that printer
    (owner or approved sender).

  - Permission requests: when you ask another user for permission to send
    to their printer, we record your user ID against that printer's
    pending-requests list until the owner approves or rejects you.

4.2 Information collected automatically

  - Authentication tokens issued by your sign-in provider so we can
    recognize you on subsequent launches without making you re-enter
    credentials.

  - Basic diagnostic information that Firebase, our backend platform,
    collects by default — including crash reports, the platform and
    version of the app you are using, and broad usage events. We do not
    use this information for advertising and we do not link it to any
    advertising identifier.

4.3 Information we do NOT collect

  - We do not collect your precise location.
  - We do not collect your contacts, calendar, or microphone input.
  - We do not collect any biometric data.
  - We do not collect health, financial, or government-ID information.
  - We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to anyone.
  - We do not show advertising and do not share data with advertisers.


5. WHY WE NEED EACH PERMISSION

Android and iOS will prompt you for several permissions. Here is exactly
what each one is for and what we do with it:

  Camera
      Used only when you choose to attach a photo to a message by taking a
      new picture in the app. The camera is opened on-demand when you tap
      the "Photo" option on the Send screen; we never access the camera
      in the background or without your interaction. Photos taken this
      way are sent only to the printer you address them to and to your
      own message history; they are stored on our backend in Firebase
      Cloud Storage and Firestore. We do not analyze image content with
      any machine learning system.

  Photo library / Photos
      Used only when you choose an existing photo from your device's
      photo library to attach to a message. We read only the specific
      photo you select; we do not scan or index your library.

  Bluetooth
      Used only during printer pairing. When you tap "Pair a printer,"
      the app scans for nearby Printimate printers advertising over
      Bluetooth Low Energy and lets you securely send your home Wi-Fi
      credentials to the printer so it can connect to the internet.
      Bluetooth is not used to track your location, to scan for non-
      Printimate devices for any other purpose, or to identify other
      Bluetooth devices around you.

  Internet / Network access
      Required for all of the app's core functions — sending messages,
      signing in, and receiving software updates.

  Notifications (Android 13+, iOS)
      Used to notify you when someone you've approved sends you a new
      message. You can disable notifications at any time in your device
      settings.


6. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use the information described in Section 4 for the following purposes:

  a) To operate the Service — authenticate you, route your messages to the
     correct printer, deliver messages to your printer, and let you manage
     who can send you messages.

  b) To maintain the security and integrity of the Service — detect abuse,
     diagnose crashes, and prevent unauthorized access to printers.

  c) To communicate with you about the Service — for example, to respond to
     a support request you send to printimately@gmail.com.

We do not use your personal information for advertising, profiling, or any
purpose other than what is described above. We do not sell your personal
information.


7. HOW YOUR INFORMATION IS SHARED

We share information only with the third-party service providers we use to
run the Service. None of these providers are permitted to use your data for
their own purposes other than as described in their own privacy policies.

  - Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore Database, Cloud Storage,
    Crashlytics, Analytics): hosts your account record, your messages,
    your printer pairings, and our diagnostic data. Subject to Google's
    privacy policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

  - Google Sign-In: if you sign in with Google, Google receives the fact
    that you used your Google account to authenticate with Printimate.
    Subject to Google's privacy policy.

  - Apple Sign in with Apple: if you sign in with Apple, Apple receives
    the fact that you used your Apple ID to authenticate with Printimate.
    Subject to Apple's privacy policy at https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/.

  - Heroku (Salesforce): hosts our backend API server, which receives
    messages from the app and writes them to Firestore. Heroku does not
    have access to your account password or sign-in credentials. Subject
    to Salesforce's privacy notice at https://www.salesforce.com/company/privacy/.

We may also disclose your information if we are legally required to do so
by a valid court order, subpoena, or law-enforcement request, or if we
believe disclosure is necessary to investigate or prevent serious harm to
any person.


8. DATA RETENTION

  - Account information is retained for as long as your account exists.
    If you delete your account, your profile record is permanently
    deleted from our active systems within 30 days. Backups may persist
    for up to 90 days before being overwritten in the normal course of
    business.

  - Message content is retained until you delete it, your account is
    deleted, or the owner of the receiving printer revokes your access.

  - Diagnostic and crash data are retained for up to 90 days, then
    automatically deleted.

To request account deletion, email printimately@gmail.com from the email
address associated with your account.


9. DATA SECURITY

We use industry-standard measures to protect your information:

  - All network traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in
    transit using TLS (HTTPS).
  - Stored data in Firestore is protected by access rules that limit who
    can read or write specific records — for example, only the owner of
    a printer can approve incoming send requests for that printer.
  - Authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication, which Google
    operates with industry-standard cryptographic safeguards.

No system is perfectly secure. While we take reasonable steps to protect
your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security and you use the
Service at your own risk.


10. YOUR RIGHTS

Depending on where you live, you may have specific rights regarding your
personal information. We offer the following rights to all users
regardless of location:

  - Access: you can request a copy of the personal information we hold
    about you.
  - Correction: you can ask us to correct information that is inaccurate.
  - Deletion: you can ask us to delete your account and the personal
    information associated with it (subject to Section 8).
  - Opt-out of diagnostics: you can request that Firebase Analytics and
    crash reporting be disabled for your account.
  - Withdraw consent: you can stop using the Service at any time by
    signing out and uninstalling the app.

To exercise any of these rights, email printimately@gmail.com. We will
respond within 30 days. We will never charge you a fee for exercising a
privacy right.

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom,
or Switzerland, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your
national data-protection authority.

If you are a resident of California, you have the additional rights
described in the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California
Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to opt out of any "sale"
or "sharing" of your personal information. We do not sell or share your
personal information as those terms are defined under California law.
